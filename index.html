Model-Specific Considerations
Four Areas Unique to This Partnership
Charity Governance

Donation Flow & Transparency

The collect-then-donate structure — where Torch pools merchant fees and forwards amounts to NGOs — requires formal Commercial Participation Agreements, quarterly donation reporting, and independent audit rights. UK charity regulation expects clear public disclosure of how much, when, and to whom donations are made.

AML / CTF

NGO Payment Flows

Pooled funds forwarded to NGOs across jurisdictions creates heightened AML/CTF sensitivity. Both parties need to demonstrate to Stripe and regulators that they understand fund flows end-to-end — including the donation component — and have appropriate monitoring in place.

UK Regulation

Consumer Protection & Surcharge Rules

UK surcharge regulations and ASA advertising guidance both apply to how Torch's fees are communicated to merchants and cardholders. Clear, non-misleading disclosure of pricing structure and donation mechanics is a legal requirement, not just good practice.

Upstream Dependency

Stripe & Card Scheme Obligations

NeroPay operates under Stripe's Scheme Services Agreement. Elevated chargebacks, consumer-harm concerns, or regulatory scrutiny flowing from Torch-level issues could prompt Stripe to review NeroPay's third-party risk posture. Visa/Mastercard rules on honest merchant pricing apply throughout the chain.
